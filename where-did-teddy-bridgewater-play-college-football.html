Where did Teddy Bridgewater play college football?

Theodore “Teddy” Edmond Bridgewater Jr. is an American football quarterback for the Miami Dolphins of the National Football League (NFL).

Where did Teddy Bridgewater play college football?

Teddy Bridgewater attended Miami Northwestern Senior High School. He was named second-team All-State for 6A classification and first-team All-County by the Miami Herald.

He was regarded as a four-star recruit and was considered the sixth-best dual-threat quarterback prospect in the nation by Rivals.com.

Initially,  he committed to the University of Miami in June 2010. However, after Miami head coach Randy Shannon, was fired in November 2010, he switched his commitment to the University of Louisville.

In his freshman year, he was named the Big East Rookie of the Year and was also named a freshman All-American by Rivals.com, Scout.com, CBS Sports, and Sporting News.

During his sophomore year in 2012, he started 11 of 12 regular season games. He was named the Big East Offensive Player of the Year.
